# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, founders, and senior engineers evaluating Rohit Mudili for AI/ML
engineering roles and contract work. They land here from LinkedIn, a resume link, or
a referral — usually skimming on a laptop between other tabs, sometimes on a phone.
The job to be done: in under 30 seconds, decide "this person builds real, shipped AI
systems and has the rigor I need." They are technical enough to recognize that "RAG
with FAISS over 1,000+ docs" and "<600ms voice-agent latency" are real claims, and
allergic to portfolios that are all gloss and no substance.

## Product Purpose

A single-page portfolio that proves Rohit is a working AI engineer — not a student
with a template. It exists to convert a cold skim into a contact: a reply, a call, a
download of the resume. Success is the visitor reaching the contact section convinced
the work is real and the craft is high. The design itself is evidence of the rigor the
copy claims.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **precise, structural, confident.**

Voice is that of an engineer who measures twice and ships. It does not oversell with
hype adjectives; it states what was built, at what scale, with what result, and lets
the numbers carry the weight. The interface should feel like standing at a drafting
table looking at a clean technical drawing — every line intentional, every label
placed, nothing decorative-for-its-own-sake. Emotionally: calm authority. The visitor
should feel they are looking at the work of someone who is exact.

## Anti-references

- **The dark-neon "AI/cyberpunk dev" portfolio** (what this currently is): black
  background, cyan/magenta gradient text, glow shadows, gradient-on-hover titles.
  This is the saturated first-order reflex for "AI engineer." Avoid entirely.
- **The editorial-magazine escape hatch**: display serif + italic + small mono
  eyebrows + ruled three-column grid. The second-order reflex. Not this either.
- **The SaaS hero-metric template**: giant gradient number, tiny label, three stat
  cards in a row. Cliché.
- **Identical icon-heading-text card grids** repeated for every section.
- **Cream/sand/parchment warm-neutral body background** — the 2026 AI default.

## Design Principles

1. **The design is the proof.** Rigor is claimed in copy and demonstrated in craft.
   Precision of alignment, label, and motion is the argument. Sloppy pixels would
   contradict the resume.
2. **Drawing, not decoration.** Every visual element behaves like part of a technical
   drawing — coordinate ticks, dimension lines, plotted nodes, schematics. Motion is
   the act of *drawing* (lines extending, points plotting), never gratuitous flourish.
3. **Numbers are the hero, not adjectives.** 1,200+ workflows, 90% reuse, <600ms,
   Top 5, Top 100. Typography and layout serve the metrics.
4. **Maximal through precision, not noise.** Ambition shows as orchestrated, exact
   motion and confident scale — not clutter, glow, or saturation.
5. **One ink, one paper, one accent.** Restraint in palette so the structure reads.
   Commitment in type scale and motion so it never reads as timid.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥ 4.5:1; large/display text ≥ 3:1 against paper.
- Ink-on-paper palette is inherently high-contrast; verify the accent and muted-label
  tones explicitly.
- Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support: the self-drawing line-work and scroll motion
  must degrade to a static, already-complete drawing (no content gated behind motion).
- Keyboard-navigable nav, form, and links; visible focus rings drawn in the accent.
- Respect color independence: never encode meaning in the accent alone (pair with
  label/position).
